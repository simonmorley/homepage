BackgroundThe short version

I've been building and breaking things for over twenty years. Started with startups — founded companies in WiFi infrastructure, contactless payments, and food tech before most people had heard of the sharing economy. Along the way I went deep on networking, wrote custom OpenWRT packages in C, and managed thousands of devices across the UK.

I moved into crypto as CTO of Vaultoro, where I rewrote their trading platform, built compliance systems from scratch, wrote Solidity contracts for ETH and ERC20 processing, and helped launch a stablecoin. I'm a published researcher and Techstars Berlin 2017 alumni.

I once built a product called Rascal — a WiFi probe listener for analytics. It worked brilliantly and would have made money. I killed it when I realised how invasive it was. I also figured out how to let Meraki customers flash their access points with our firmware, which made Cisco rather unhappy.

I took a year off to train as a professional Ironman triathlete — swim, bike, run — and then COVID killed the race calendar. These days I keep bees on an allotment and built a monitor for the hives with a Raspberry Pi. I still couldn't finish Ulysses.
